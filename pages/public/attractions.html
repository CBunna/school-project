Beskydy Tourist Attractions

Discover the most beautiful places in our region

Main Attractions

Beskydy offers countless beautiful places, historical monuments and natural attractions. Explore our most popular destinations.

Lysá Hora (4,341 ft)

The highest peak of the Moravian-Silesian Beskydy and the third highest mountain in the Czech Republic outside the Krkonoše range. At the summit there is a mountain hut and a stone lookout tower called Šindler's Tower, from which there is a magnificent view as far as the Tatras.

  • Ascent from Ostravice (blue trail) - approx. 3 hours
  • Cable car access from Ostravice - fast and convenient
  • Lookout tower with views of 4 countries (in good weather)
  • Mountain hut with refreshments
  • Suitable for families with children and demanding hikers
GPS: 49.5456°N, 18.4475°E

Pustevny

Mountain saddle at an altitude of 3,340 ft, where unique buildings designed by architect Dušan Jurkovič are located. The Libušín and Maměnka buildings represent the pinnacle of Wallachian wooden architecture and are protected cultural monuments.

  • Unique architecture in Art Nouveau and folk art style
  • Restaurant with traditional Wallachian cuisine
  • Hotel for tourist accommodation
  • Starting point for Radhošť (45 minutes walk)
  • Accessible by cable car from Trojanovic
  • Popular venue for weddings and events
GPS: 49.4967°N, 18.2569°E

Radhošť (3,704 ft)

Mythical mountain associated with the Slavic legend of the god Radegast. At the summit stands the neo-Gothic chapel of St. Cyril and Methodius and the monumental statue of Radegast by Albín Polášek. Important pilgrimage and tourist site.

  • Spiritual center of Moravia
  • Chapel built in 1898
  • Statue of Radegast (1931) - symbol of the region
  • Mountain hut with refreshments
  • Regular pilgrimages and cultural events
  • Beautiful views of surrounding mountains
GPS: 49.4914°N, 18.2503°E

More Interesting Places

Troják (Trojačka)

Junction of three hiking trails on the Beskydy ridge with beautiful views. Ideal place to rest during a trek.

49.5125 18.3869

Elevation: 3,816 ft

Difficulty: Moderate

Prašivá

Third highest mountain of Beskydy (4,357 ft) on the Slovak side. Meteorological station and TV transmitter dominate the summit.

49.2956 19.0947

Elevation: 4,357 ft

Difficulty: Challenging

Smrk

Dominant feature of the western part of Beskydy with a lookout tower providing 360° views. Popular destination for tourists even in winter.

49.6764 18.4769

Elevation: 4,186 ft

Difficulty: Moderate

Velká Čantoryje

Easternmost peak of Beskydy on the Polish-Czech border. Exceptional view of the Ostrava basin and Polish Beskydy.

49.6850 18.7550

Elevation: 3,264 ft

Difficulty: Easy to Moderate

Velký Javorník

Mountain with TV transmitter and lookout tower. Accessible by car almost to the summit, popular among less fit tourists.

49.4542 18.5517

Elevation: 3,514 ft

Difficulty: Easy

Kněhyně

Forested summit with a tourist hut. Starting point for ridge treks and crossings to other parts of Beskydy.

49.4858 18.4136

Elevation: 4,124 ft

Difficulty: Moderate

Cultural Monuments

🏛️ Wallachian Open-Air Museum

The largest open-air museum in the Czech Republic in Rožnov pod Radhoštěm. Unique collection of folk architecture and demonstrations of traditional crafts.

Opening Hours: April - October (daily)

Admission: 200 CZK adults, 120 CZK children

Official website →

🏰 Hukvaldy Castle

Extensive castle ruins on a rocky promontory. Birthplace of composer Leoš Janáček. Regular cultural events and concerts.

Opening Hours: May - September (daily)

Admission: 80 CZK adults, 50 CZK children

⛪ Wooden Churches

Unique wooden sacral buildings in many Beskydy villages. Some listed on the UNESCO list (e.g. Hrčava, Hervartov).

Type: Cultural monuments

Access: Varies by church
